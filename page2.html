Memorizing Logical Subjects!

Logical subjects like Math, Physics, and Chemistry challenge your reasoning and problem-solving skills rather than relying solely on memorization. Mastering these subjects requires a clear understanding of concepts, regular practice, and the ability to apply knowledge in different scenarios. On this page, explore proven strategies to help you grasp complex topics, solve problems efficiently, and build confidence in tackling logical subjects.


Consistency is key in logical subjects. Dedicate time daily to practice problems and sample tests, starting with easier ones and gradually moving to more challenging questions. This not only helps improve your skills but also builds confidence. When solving problems, always break them into smaller, manageable steps and write everything clearly. This will help you avoid mistakes and understand the logic behind each step.

Memorization isn’t entirely avoidable in logical subjects, but it should be done strategically. Focus on memorizing key formulas and rules only after you understand how they’re derived. Tools like flashcards or mnenomics (see page 1 for more details) can also help you recall information faster during exams. Don’t fear mistakes; they are excellent opportunities to learn. Analyze errors in your problem-solving process and rework the question from scratch instead of just correcting it. This approach ensures you fully grasp the concept and won’t repeat the same errors.

Active studying is more effective than passive reading. Solve questions as you study instead of just reading through examples or solutions. Working with peers can also make a big difference. Group discussions allow you to learn different approaches (don't chit chat tho, lol), and teaching someone else is one of the best ways to solidify your knowledge.

Finally, remember to take care of your mental energy and health. Logical thinking can be draining, so incorporate short breaks into your study routine to refresh your mind. A clear and focused approach, combined with consistent practice, will help you excel in these challenging but rewarding subjects. And remember students, sleeping is important! Don't pull out all nighters.
