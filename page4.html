Useful Websites for Students

Discover resources that make studying effective and enjoyable. These websites cater to different subjects and skills to help students achieve academic success.


Khan Academy

Khan Academy is an incredible resource offering free courses, lessons, and practice exercises for students of all ages. It covers a wide range of subjects, including mathematics, science, economics, computing, and the humanities. The platform features detailed video tutorials, interactive quizzes, and progress tracking tools to help learners master concepts step by step. Whether you’re preparing for standardized tests or brushing up on specific topics, Khan Academy provides a personalized learning experience tailored to your pace.

Quizlet

Quizlet transforms the way students study by providing innovative tools like flashcards, games, and quizzes. Users can create their own study sets or access millions of pre-made decks spanning various topics and subjects. With engaging features such as “Learn Mode” and “Test Mode,” studying becomes more interactive and less boring. The platform is perfect for memorizing vocabulary, formulas, or historical dates.

Forest App

Forest App helps users stay focused by turning productivity into a game. Using the Pomodoro technique, you plant a virtual tree that grows as you stay focused and withers if you leave the app. The more you focus, the bigger your virtual forest gets. In addition to boosting focus, Forest also helps the environment. With progress tracking, customizable timers, and focus challenges, Forest turns staying on task into a rewarding experience, making it perfect for students and anyone looking to improve productivity.

Coursera

Coursera offers access to online courses taught by professors from world-renowned universities such as Stanford, Yale, and Harvard. With courses spanning every subject imaginable—business, data science, arts, and more—students can develop new skills or dive deeper into their academic interests. Coursera also provides certifications upon course completion, which can enhance your resume/CV if you're a college/university student.

Grammarly

Grammarly is the ultimate writing assistant for students. It goes beyond basic spelling and grammar checks by offering suggestions for improving clarity, tone, and style. Whether you’re drafting an essay, writing an email, or creating a report, Grammarly ensures your writing is polished and professional. With browser extensions and mobile apps, Grammarly supports writing across various platforms. (Warning : Don't rely on it too much for your essays.)

Notion

Notion is an all-in-one workspace for note-taking, task management, and collaboration. It allows users to create customized pages for different purposes, such as to-do lists, databases, wikis, and calendars. Notion’s flexibility makes it ideal for students, offering tools for organizing notes, tracking assignments, and managing projects. Its clean, minimalist design ensures that users stay focused and organized.
